Daniel C. Narey

Daniel Narey is the man behind Visotype, where for the time being he wears all of the hats — designer, developer, creative. An ex-academic turned technologist, Daniel is driven by intellectual curiosity, dissatisfaction with mediocrity, and a belief in the transformative power of good design. He is based in the East End neighborhood of Pittsburgh, PA, a rapidly growing urban technology hub.

As a PhD student studying social science, research methodology, and research education, Daniel found himself frustrated with the limited impact of academic knowledge and the extent to which intellectual conventions, methodological habits, and forms of scholarly communication remain stuck in the 20th century. After beginning to grasp how much data science, machine learning, and modern web technologies are transforming industry and culture, Daniel decided to join the 21st century — his new vocation would be to apply his critical thinking abilities, systematic design skills, and deep understanding of knowledge architecture toward the development of products that help people make sense of data and communicate insights to others.

Statistical computing has grown by leaps and bounds over the last 20 years, but even within the academic research community, relatively few people have the programming facility required to make use of this growing corpus of computational tools. While there are some good all-around software packages on the market with graphical user interfaces for data analysis and visualization, full-featured versions of these products are prohibitively expensive for many individuals and organizations. Rather than trying to build data analysis software that does everything, Daniel’s vision for Visotype is to create a library of small, well-designed analytics and visualization tools, focused on meeting the needs of particular types of users. All Visotype software tools will be released under a GPLv3 open source license, which means they may be freely downloaded, run on local machines, and modified by users with the requisite expertise. Visotype will make money by selling subscriptions to a cloud platform where users can access the full library of software tools, upload their data, run computations on fast servers, and store analytics reports and visualizations. This business model is known as OpenSaaS.
